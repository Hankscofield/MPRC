Personal Safety Details

  • Inspect before use: Check equipment for damage, loose bolts, or wear before using. Report any issues to MPRC.
  • Understand the equipment: If you're not sure how to use something, observe others or look for instructional signs.
  • Warm up first: Cold muscles are injury-prone. Do light warm-up exercises before using resistance equipment.
  • Know your limits: Don't attempt exercises beyond your current ability. Build up gradually.
  • Maintain control: Move through exercises with control, not momentum. This is safer and more effective.
  • Watch your surroundings: Be aware of space around you, especially when using equipment that involves swinging or extending limbs.

Sharing Equipment

  • Reasonable time limits: When others are waiting, limit yourself to 10-15 minutes per piece of equipment, or 2-3 sets of exercises.
  • Work in: It's acceptable to ask "Mind if I work in?" when someone is between sets. Alternate sets efficiently.
  • Don't monopolize: Avoid "circuiting" through multiple pieces of equipment in a way that ties them all up.
  • Yield to others: If you're resting, don't sit on equipment. Step away so others can use it.
  • Help if asked: If someone asks for a spot or how to use equipment, help if you're able.

Hygiene and Maintenance

  • Wipe equipment: If you sweat heavily, consider bringing a towel to wipe handles after use.
  • Report damage: If you notice damaged or dangerous equipment, report it through MPRC immediately.
  • Don't modify: Never attempt to adjust, repair, or modify park equipment yourself.
  • No food or drinks: Keep food and sugary drinks away from equipment to prevent attracting pests and creating mess.
